### 1. Verdict
The structure is exceptionally well-organized for LLM-human collaboration, prioritizing core "contracts" at the root which makes the system's "personality" and rules immediately accessible. It feels like a living system rather than just a code repository, though it could lean further into human-intuitive naming and cross-platform accessibility to be truly "ready for real project use."

### 2. Strongest points
*   **Root-level Constitutions:** Placing `AGENTS.md` and `GEMINI.md` at the root is a brilliant UX choice; it signals that the "who" and "how" are as important as the "what," fostering a sense of partnership.
*   **Audit vs. Process Separation:** Storing raw outputs separately from normalized reviews is a high-integrity design that protects the orchestrator's context from noise while maintaining full transparency.
*   **Temporal Narrative:** The date-prefixed task folders in `runs/` provide a clear, chronological "memory" of the project's evolution that is easy for humans to navigate.

### 3. Risks / weaknesses
*   **Technical Jargon Barrier:** Terms like "packets" and "orchestration" are precise but cold; they may create a higher cognitive entry barrier for non-technical users compared to more human terms like "briefs" or "collabs."
*   **Platform Friction:** The reliance on `.sh` scripts in the `scripts/` directory may alienate Windows users (like the current environment), conflicting with a "human-centered" goal of universal accessibility.
*   **Navigation Fatigue:** Without a high-level index, the `runs/` folder will eventually become a "wall of folders," making it difficult for humans to quickly find past decisions without digging through subdirectories.

### 4. Recommended changes
*   **Cross-platform Runner:** Replace or supplement `.sh` scripts with a universal runner (e.g., Python or Node) to ensure the system is accessible across all operating systems.
*   **Add a `runs/INDEX.md`:** Maintain a simple, top-level list of tasks, their status, and key decisions to provide a "human-friendly" entry point to the project's history.
*   **Human-centric Aliasing:** In documentation, consider aliasing "packets" to "briefings" or "decision briefs" to make the framework feel more like a team collaboration and less like a technical protocol.
*   **Root `README.md`:** Introduce a human-focused entry point that explains the *intent* and *vision* of the project, distinct from the LLM-focused `CLAUDE.md`.
